I am an assistant professor (full-time faculty member) in the Faculty of Computer Science and Artifical Intelligence at Shenzhen University of Advanced Technology (SUAT). 
I got my Ph.D. degree and M.Eng degree at the College of Intelligence and Computing in [Tianjin University](http://www.tju.edu.cn/) (TJU) under the supervision of Prof. [Wei Feng](http://cic.tju.edu.cn/faculty/fengwei/index.html), in 2023 and 2019, respectively. I was a research fellow at Shenshen Institutes of Advanced Technology and City University of Hong Kong.

Now, I serve as the PI and lead the Raise Lab (**R**esearch on **AI** **SE**eing Laboratory). I am authorized to supervise Ph.D. and Master students in Computer Science. My research interest is visual intelligence, specifically including visual perception, understanding and generation, and the Unified Multimodal Models (UMMs) of them, multi-camera/agent visual collaborative analysis, open-world/vocabulary object perception, and the world models.

I am lucky to have opportunities to work closely with Prof. [Song Wang](University of South Carolina and now in SUAT)， Prof. [Liang Wan](http://cic.tju.edu.cn/faculty/lwan/index.html) (Tianjin University) and Prof. [Junhui Hou](http://www.cityu.edu.hk/stfprofile/csjhhou.htm) (CityU Hong Kong).
I am also happy to cooperate with my excellent junior fellow apprentices [Jiewen Zhao](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=kjDZaX8AAAAJ&hl=zh-CN), [Yiyang Gan](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=TO3J9vgAAAAJ&hl=zh-CN), [Jiacheng Li](https://scholar.google.com.hk/citations?user=FDDnwyYAAAAJ&hl=zh-CN), Haomin Yan, Liqiang Yin, [Zekun Qian](https://scholar.google.com.hk/citations?user=ZxANT60AAAAJ&hl=zh-CN), [Xiangqun Zhang](https://scholar.google.com.hk/citations?user=eVeaRp0AAAAJ&hl=zh-CN), [Feifan Wang](https://scholar.google.com.hk/citations?user=ZxANT60AAAAJ&hl=zh-CN), [Songmiao Wang](https://scholar.google.com.hk/citations?user=2buOSJsAAAAJ&hl=zh-CN), [Likai Wang](https://scholar.google.com.hk/citations?user=eayfW3sAAAAJ&hl=zh-CN).
